Event Objectives

1st WKU International Conference on Education (WKUICE) aims to provide an international platform for scholars, practitioners, and graduate students to present their ideas, explore issues within their specific area of expertise, and collaborate with the professionals for their future scholarly endeavors. Through engagements with scholars from different expertise within the field of education, academics and scholars can be exposed to new perspectives and create new knowledge. With its current and thought-provoking theme, the conference will spark fresh ideas and lead to research partnerships that could contribute to new perspectives.

The target audiences for WKUICE are Social Scientists, Research Scholars, teachers, educational leaders, policymakers, Students, and potentially anyone who has a stake in education in one way or the other. We truly believe that attending the conference, either as a presenter or an attendee, would be a highly enriching experience and provide diverse and fresh perspectives from the field. WKUICE intends to offer a rewarding experience for the two days of the conference with keynotes from eminent scholars, symposiums, and other scholarly activities that will keep you engaged.

Call For Papers

How would teaching and learning look like in 2040, when children born today would be entering colleges? Would there still be brick-and-mortar schools and colleges? Will there be any dramatic change in teaching as a profession? These are difficult questions, and it is not possible to give a precise answer to these questions. With sustained efforts from the government in most countries, more and more people are getting educated now. There is a great thirst for education that would equip students for the challenges of the 21st century. However, we are attempting to prepare students for a future world, the shape of which is not easy to perceive at this moment.

The pandemic has revigorated the persistent calls for addressing the disparities in education. While there is a consensus that for students to succeed in the future world, specific skills – called 21st-century skills – are required, there is little known about what shape and form these skills are to be taught or manifested. It is time to rethink and reimagine an education system that caters to the present and future educational needs, for which students have already begun preparing in their early years of schooling.

Thus, the theme for this conference – Reimagining education for the 21st century is pertinent and timely. The conference intends to provide a forum for frank and open discussions to continue the ongoing in different parts of the world. We encourage presenters to approach their presentations from multiple perspectives and for every level of education, be it K-12, Undergraduate, post-graduate, or polytechnic colleges.

Event Details

The two-day conference will be held on Saturday and Sunday, 23rd and 24th April 2021. Due to COVID-related travel restrictions, we have decided to organize this conference virtually. We are confident that our 2nd conference in April 2023 will be entirely in-person, where the participants will get the opportunity to meet other scholars in person.

There will be three keynote addresses by eminent scholars from the field of education. We will also conduct a symposium entitled ‘Publishing in High-Impact International Journals’, which will provide crucial information on publishing in high-impact journals. Senior editors of three international journals will participate in the symposium.

A detailed schedule for the conference will be posted on this page in March when we have received and accepted the submissions. So watch out for this space.

Places to Explore

Originally known as Yongjia (Chinese: 永嘉), Wenzhou is the 15th largest city in China and is considered the manufacturing hub of China. It is located in the southeastern part of China, in the Zhejiang province. Zhejiang province is the southernmost province of China, mostly mountainous and lies along the East China Sea. The population of Wenzhou is 9.2 million people, with more than 3 million living in its urban areas. The city is well connected with other parts of the country through high-speed trains, buses, and planes. With the high-speed train, it takes a little more than three hours to Shanghai. The city has an excellent public transport system with buses connecting every part of the city. There is a metro line as well, called S1, while two additional lines are under construction. Wenzhou-Kean University

The Wenzhou-Kean University was established in 1941 as Delhi Polytechnic by the government of India and became Delhi College of Engineering (DCE) in 1925. In 2009 the college was given state university status, thus changing it's name to Delhi Technological University. DTU is a technical Institute of excellence in higher education in Engineering as well as specialized areas of Applied Sciences. It is one of the oldest Engineering Colleges in India and Delhi's first Engineering College. University provides comprehensive opportunities to it's faculties to explore and attain the zenith of success in their respective fields. The main objective of the University is to integrate the development process of various industries and academia.
